# Hemoglobin (Hb)

## What It Measures
Hemoglobin is an iron-rich protein in your red blood cells. It carries oxygen from your lungs to the rest of your body and brings carbon dioxide back to the lungs to be exhaled.

## Why is it Important?
Without enough hemoglobin, your organs and tissues won't get the oxygen they need to function optimally, leading to fatigue and weakness.

## Normal Ranges
- **Adult Male:** 13.0 - 17.0 g/dL
- **Adult Female:** 12.0 - 15.5 g/dL
*Note: In the Indian context, averages often trend slightly lower due to widespread dietary iron deficiency.*

## What HIGH Hemoglobin Means
A high hemoglobin level (polycythemia) means your blood is thicker than normal. 
- **Common causes:** Dehydration (very common), smoking, living at high altitudes.
- **Medical causes:** Lung disease, congenital heart disease, or polycythemia vera (a bone marrow disorder).

## What LOW Hemoglobin Means
A low level indicates **Anemia**.
- **Common causes in India:** Iron deficiency (poor diet or poor absorption), Vitamin B12 or folate deficiency.
- **Other causes:** Blood loss (heavy menstruation, ulcers), chronic kidney disease, or chronic inflammation.

## Lifestyle & Diet Recommendations
### If Low (Anemia Profile):
- **Increase Iron Intake:** Eat more spinach (palak), beetroot, dates, jaggery (gud), legumes (chana/rajma), and lean meats.
- **Cooking Tip:** Cook acidic foods (like tomatoes) in cast-iron cookware to increase iron content.
- **Absorption Booster:** Pair iron-rich foods with Vitamin C (lemon juice, amla, oranges) for better absorption.
- **Things to Avoid:** Do NOT drink tea or coffee within 1 hour of meals (tannins block iron absorption).

### If High:
- **Hydration:** Ensure you are drinking 2.5 - 3 liters of water daily.
- **Quit Smoking:** If you smoke, quitting will help normalize levels.

## Questions to Ask Your Doctor
- "Should I get a complete Iron Profile (Serum Iron, Ferritin, TIBC) or Vitamin B12 test to find the exact cause?"
- "Do you recommend iron supplements, and if so, what dosage?"
- "Could my current medications be affecting my hemoglobin levels?"
