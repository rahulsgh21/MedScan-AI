# Diabetes & Blood Sugar Biomarkers

## Overview
Diabetes is a metabolic disorder where blood glucose levels remain chronically elevated because the body either doesn't make enough insulin, or doesn't use it effectively. India has the second-largest diabetic population in the world (~101 million as of 2023). Pre-diabetes affects an even larger population. Early detection and lifestyle changes are critical.

---

## Fasting Blood Sugar (FBS) / Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG)

### What it measures
Blood glucose level after at least 8-10 hours of fasting (no food or drinks except water).

### Normal Range
| Category | FBS Value |
|---|---|
| Normal | 70 - 99 mg/dL |
| Prediabetes | 100 - 125 mg/dL |
| Diabetes | ≥ 126 mg/dL (on two separate tests) |

### HIGH FBS means
- **Prediabetes (100-125):** Insulin resistance is developing. This is REVERSIBLE with lifestyle changes.
- **Diabetes (≥126):** Either Type 1 (rare, usually younger) or Type 2 (very common in India, especially with family history, overweight, sedentary lifestyle).

### LOW FBS (Hypoglycemia, <70 mg/dL)
In diabetic patients: Too much insulin medication, skipped meal, excess exercise.
In non-diabetic patients: Rare — investigate for insulin-producing tumors (insulinoma), severe liver disease, or starvation.

## Post-Prandial Blood Sugar (PP Blood Sugar / PPBS)

### What it measures
Blood glucose 2 hours after eating a full meal. Tests how well the body manages a glucose "load".

### Normal Range
| Category | PPBS Value |
|---|---|
| Normal | < 140 mg/dL |
| Prediabetes | 140 - 199 mg/dL |
| Diabetes | ≥ 200 mg/dL |

### Why it matters
Some people have normal fasting blood sugar but cannot handle the glucose spike from a meal — PPBS catches this. This is called **Impaired Glucose Tolerance** and is a form of prediabetes.

---

## HbA1c (Glycosylated Hemoglobin / Hemoglobin A1c)

### What it measures
Hemoglobin that has glucose attached to it. Since red blood cells live for ~3 months, HbA1c reflects your AVERAGE blood sugar over the past 2-3 months. A single FBS test can be misleading (one bad day). HbA1c gives the "big picture."

### Normal Range
| Category | HbA1c Value |
|---|---|
| Normal | < 5.7% |
| Prediabetes | 5.7% - 6.4% |
| Diabetic target (managed) | < 7.0% |
| Poorly controlled Diabetes | ≥ 8.0% |

### About HbA1c in India
Indian patients often have higher HbA1c values for the same blood glucose readings compared to Western populations — a phenomenon related to genetic differences in red blood cell turnover. Discuss with your doctor if you are of Indian heritage.

### Making sense of the number:
- HbA1c of 6.4% ≈ average blood sugar of ~137 mg/dL
- HbA1c of 7.0% ≈ average blood sugar of ~154 mg/dL
- HbA1c of 8.0% ≈ average blood sugar of ~183 mg/dL

## Insulin Resistance and the Metabolic Syndrome

Many Indians have "thin-fat" syndrome — they look slim but have excess visceral (belly) fat and are insulin resistant. This leads to:
- High blood sugar (prediabetes)
- High triglycerides
- Low HDL cholesterol
- High blood pressure
- Abdominal obesity

This cluster is called **Metabolic Syndrome** and significantly increases cardiovascular risk.

## Lifestyle Tips for Blood Sugar Control

### Diet
- **Reduce Glycemic Load:** Switch from white rice to brown rice, ragi, bajra, jowar, or millets. These are traditional Indian grains that are now being rediscovered for their low glycemic index.
- **Portion control:** Use smaller plates. If eating rice, treat it as a side dish rather than the main dish.
- **Fibre first:** Start meals with salad or vegetables before carbohydrates to slow absorption.
- **Avoid liquid sugar:** Limit fruit juices, soft drinks, packaged beverages. Eat whole fruits instead.
- **Limit maida:** Restrict white flour-based foods (bread, biscuits, samosas, naan).
- **Healthy fats in moderation:** Add nuts (almonds, walnuts), seeds, and avocado.

### Physical Activity
- **After-meal walks:** A 15-minute brisk walk after each major meal is one of the most effective ways to lower post-prandial blood sugar spikes.
- **Resistance training:** Building muscle mass improves long-term insulin sensitivity.
- Aim for 150 minutes of aerobic activity per week.

### Monitoring
- If newly diagnosed with prediabetes or diabetes, self-monitoring with a glucometer is recommended.
- Keep a food diary for at least 2 weeks to identify which foods cause the biggest spikes.

### Stress and Sleep
- Chronic stress elevates cortisol which directly raises blood sugar. Practice yoga, meditation, or deep breathing.
- Poor sleep (under 6 hours) acutely worsens insulin resistance.

## Questions to Ask Your Doctor
- "Based on my FBS and HbA1c together, am I prediabetic or diabetic?"
- "Is metformin or any medication indicated, or can we try lifestyle changes first?"
- "Should I check my blood sugar 2 hours after meals (post-prandial) as well?"
- "Do I need to see a diabetic educator or dietician for a personalised meal plan?"
- "Should I also check my kidney function (microalbuminuria) given my blood sugar levels?"
