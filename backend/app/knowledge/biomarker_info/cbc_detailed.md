# Complete Blood Count (CBC) — Detailed Biomarkers

## Overview
The CBC (Complete Blood Count) is the most commonly ordered lab test. It measures the three main types of cells in blood: Red Blood Cells (RBCs), White Blood Cells (WBCs), and Platelets. The CBC gives a snapshot of overall health and can detect many conditions including anemia, infection, and blood cancers.

---

## White Blood Cell (WBC) Count

### Normal Range: 4,000 - 10,000 cells/cumm

### HIGH WBC (Leukocytosis) means
The immune system is active or stressed.
- **Bacterial infection** (most common — pneumonia, UTI, appendicitis)
- **Stress** (physical or emotional — even strenuous exercise can raise it temporarily)
- **Allergies or asthma** (especially if Eosinophils are also high)
- **Steroid medications** (like prednisolone) can raise WBC
- Rarely: Leukemia (especially if very high and unexplained)

### LOW WBC (Leukopenia) means
- Viral infections (e.g., dengue — very common in India)
- Certain medications (chemotherapy, some antibiotics)
- Bone marrow problems
- Nutritional deficiencies (B12, folate)

---

## WBC Differential Count

### Neutrophils (Normal: 40-80%)
The first responders to bacterial infections. **HIGH neutrophils = bacterial infection or inflammation.**

### Lymphocytes (Normal: 20-40%)
Responsible for viral immunity. **HIGH lymphocytes = viral infection (dengue, flu, COVID-19).** **LOW lymphocytes = HIV, or severe stress on the immune system.**

### Eosinophils (Normal: 1-6%)
Fight parasites and are involved in allergic reactions. **HIGH eosinophils (>6%) = Allergies (asthma, allergic rhinitis), or parasitic infection (worm infestations — common in India).**

### Monocytes (Normal: 2-10%)
Clean up after infections. Mildly elevated monocytes are common and often not significant.

### Basophils (Normal: 0-2%)
Rare to be elevated. Very HIGH basophils can suggest a bone marrow condition.

## RBC Count (Red Blood Cells)

### Normal Range
- Male: 4.5 - 5.5 million/cumm
- Female: 4.0 - 5.0 million/cumm

### LOW RBC → Anemia (see Hemoglobin document for full detail)
### HIGH RBC → Polycythemia (see Hemoglobin document)

---

## MCV (Mean Corpuscular Volume)

### What it measures
The average SIZE of your red blood cells. This is extremely useful for diagnosing the *type* of anemia.

### Normal Range: 83 - 101 fL

### LOW MCV (Microcytic Anemia — small red cells)
- **Iron Deficiency Anemia** — most common cause in India
- **Thalassemia Trait** — very common in certain Indian communities (Gujarati, Bengali, Punjabi, Sindhi)

### HIGH MCV (Macrocytic Anemia — large red cells)
- **Vitamin B12 Deficiency** — extremely prevalent in India, especially among vegetarians
- **Folate Deficiency**
- **Alcohol** use

### NORMAL MCV with Low Hemoglobin (Normocytic Anemia)
- Chronic kidney disease, chronic disease/inflammation, or recent blood loss

---

## MCH (Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin)

### What it measures
The average amount of hemoglobin IN each red blood cell.

### Normal Range: 27 - 32 pg

### Interpretation matches MCV:
- **Low MCH (Hypochromic):** Iron deficiency — cells are pale and small.
- **High MCH:** B12 or folate deficiency — cells are large.

---

## MCHC (Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin Concentration)

### What it measures
The concentration of hemoglobin within red blood cells.

### Normal Range: 31.5 - 34.5 g/dL

### Interpretation
Usually the last CBC parameter to fall below normal. Low MCHC confirms hypochromic (pale-colored) anemia, consistent with iron deficiency.

---

## RDW-CV (Red Cell Distribution Width)

### What it measures
How much red blood cells VARY in size. A high RDW means your RBCs are coming in many different sizes — a sign the bone marrow is struggling.

### Normal Range: 11.6 - 14.0 %

### HIGH RDW means
- **Iron deficiency anemia** (mixed sizes as new iron-starved cells are produced)
- **B12/Folate deficiency**
- **Mixed deficiency** (very common in India — e.g., both iron AND B12 deficient)
- **Early thalassemia trait**

### RDW + MCV Combination (Differential Diagnosis)
| RDW | MCV | Likely Cause |
|---|---|---|
| High | Low | Iron Deficiency Anemia |
| Normal | Low | Thalassemia Trait (likely) |
| High | High | B12 / Folate Deficiency |
| High | Normal | Mixed Deficiency (Iron + B12) |

## Platelet Count (PLC / PLT)

### Normal Range: 1,50,000 - 4,10,000 cells/cumm

### LOW Platelets (Thrombocytopenia) means
- **Dengue fever** — platelet drop is the hallmark of dengue (very important in India)
- **Viral infections** generally
- **Certain medications** (heparin, some antibiotics)
- **Immune conditions** (ITP — Immune Thrombocytopenic Purpura)
- Watch out: Below 20,000 is a risk for spontaneous bleeding.

### HIGH Platelets (Thrombocytosis) means
- Infection or inflammation (reactive thrombocytosis — common and usually harmless)
- Iron deficiency anemia
- After surgery or splenectomy
- Rarely: a bone marrow disorder

### Lifestyle Tips:
- If platelets are LOW due to Dengue: papaya leaf juice and kiwi are traditional Indian remedies. Some studies show papaya leaf extract may help platelet recovery. Stay well-hydrated.
- There are no specific dietary tips to lower high platelets — treat the underlying cause.

---

## ESR (Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate)

### What it measures
How quickly red blood cells sink to the bottom of a test tube. When inflammation or infection is present, proteins make RBCs clump and fall faster.

### Normal Range
- Male: < 15 mm/hr
- Female: < 20 mm/hr (slightly higher due to hormonal factors)

### HIGH ESR means
ESR is a non-specific marker — it just says "something is inflamed somewhere."
- **Infections:** TB (very important in India — ESR is elevated in TB), bacterial infections.
- **Autoimmune diseases:** Rheumatoid arthritis, lupus.
- **Anemia** (especially if RBC are abnormal).
- Chronic inflammatory diseases.

**Important:** ESR alone is not diagnostic. It's used alongside other tests to monitor known conditions or as a flag to investigate further.

## Questions to Ask Your Doctor
- "Do my MCV and RDW findings suggest iron deficiency or something else like thalassemia?"
- "Should I get a Vitamin B12 and Folate test to rule out nutritional anemia?"
- "My eosinophils are high — should I be tested for a parasitic infection or allergies?"
- "My platelet count is low — should I be concerned about dengue or another cause?"
- "Is my ESR elevation consistent with an infection or an autoimmune condition?"
