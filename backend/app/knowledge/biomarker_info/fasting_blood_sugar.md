# Fasting Blood Sugar (FBS)

## What It Measures
Fasting Blood Sugar tests the amount of glucose (sugar) in your blood after you haven't eaten or drunk anything (except water) for at least 8 to 12 hours.

## Why is it Important?
Glucose is a major source of energy for your cells, but it needs the hormone insulin to enter the cells. In conditions like diabetes, glucose builds up in the blood instead of going into cells, which can damage organs, nerves, and blood vessels over time.

## Normal Ranges
- **Normal:** 70 to 99 mg/dL
- **Prediabetes (Impaired Fasting Glucose):** 100 to 125 mg/dL
- **Diabetes:** 126 mg/dL or higher (on two separate tests)

## What HIGH Fasting Blood Sugar Means (Hyperglycemia)
High fasting blood sugar almost always indicates issues with insulin resistance or insulin production.
- **Prediabetes:** Your body is becoming resistant to insulin. This is a critical warning stage where lifestyle changes can reverse the trend.
- **Diabetes:** Your body isn't producing enough insulin or isn't using it effectively.
- **Other temporary causes:** Extreme stress, illness, or certain medications (like corticosteroids).

## What LOW Fasting Blood Sugar Means (Hypoglycemia)
Values below 70 mg/dL are considered low.
- **Causes:** If you are diabetic, this is usually caused by taking too much insulin or diabetes medication, skipping a meal, or exercising more than usual. In non-diabetics, it's rare but can be caused by liver disease, starvation, or rare pancreatic tumors.

## Lifestyle & Diet Recommendations
### If High (Prediabetes/Diabetes):
- **Carbohydrate Management:** Switch from simple refined carbs (white rice, white bread, maida) to complex carbs with a low glycemic index (brown rice, whole wheat rotis, ragi, bajra).
- **Avoid Added Sugars:** Eliminate sugary drinks, sweets, and excessive fruit juices.
- **Portion Control:** Follow the "plate method" (half plate vegetables, quarter plate lean protein, quarter plate complex carbs).
- **Physical Activity:** Regular exercise makes your cells more sensitive to insulin. A 15-minute walk after meals can drastically reduce blood sugar spikes.
- **Stress & Sleep:** Chronic stress and lack of sleep directly raise blood sugar levels by increasing cortisol.

### If Low:
- If you feel dizzy, sweaty, or confused, consume a fast-acting carb immediately (e.g., half cup fruit juice, 1 tablespoon sugar/honey, or hard candies). Follow up with a balanced meal.
