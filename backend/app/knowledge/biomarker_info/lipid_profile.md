# Lipid Profile Biomarkers

## Overview
A lipid profile (also called a lipid panel or cholesterol test) measures the types of fat (lipids) in your blood. It's one of the most important tests for assessing cardiovascular disease risk — heart disease is the leading cause of death in India, and dyslipidemia (abnormal cholesterol) is a major modifiable risk factor.

---

## Total Cholesterol

### What it measures
The total of all cholesterol types in the blood. Think of it as the sum of LDL, HDL, and VLDL.

### Normal Range
| Category | Total Cholesterol |
|---|---|
| Desirable | < 200 mg/dL |
| Borderline High | 200 - 239 mg/dL |
| High | ≥ 240 mg/dL |

### High Total Cholesterol → Increased risk of:
- Heart attack (myocardial infarction)
- Stroke
- Peripheral artery disease

---

## LDL Cholesterol (Low-Density Lipoprotein)

### What it measures
Often called **"bad cholesterol."** LDL carries cholesterol FROM the liver to the body. When too high, it deposits in artery walls forming **plaques** (atherosclerosis).

### Normal Range (depends on risk profile)
| Risk Level | LDL Target |
|---|---|
| Low risk (healthy person) | < 130 mg/dL |
| Moderate risk (1-2 risk factors) | < 100 mg/dL |
| High risk (diabetes, heart disease) | < 70 mg/dL |

### HIGH LDL causes:
- Diet high in saturated fats (ghee, butter, red meat, full-fat dairy) and trans fats (vanaspati, processed snacks)
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Genetics (Familial Hypercholesterolemia — runs in families)
- Hypothyroidism (thyroid issues raise LDL)
- Taking certain medications

### Lifestyle Tips if HIGH:
- **Eliminate trans fats:** Vanaspati ghee / dalda, biscuits, fried namkeen, store-bought bakery items.
- **Reduce saturated fats:** Limit excess ghee, butter, coconut oil — not eliminate entirely, but moderate.
- **Increase soluble fibre:** Oats, psyllium husk (isabgol), beans, lentils, apples — soluble fibre binds to cholesterol in the gut.
- **Plant sterols/stanols:** Found in nuts, seeds, vegetable oils — they competitively block cholesterol absorption.
- **Exercise:** Aerobic exercise helps raise HDL and can modestly lower LDL.

---

## HDL Cholesterol (High-Density Lipoprotein)

### What it measures
Often called **"good cholesterol."** HDL carries cholesterol BACK from the tissues to the liver for elimination. Think of it as the cleanup crew.

### Normal Range
- Male: > 40 mg/dL (ideal: > 60 mg/dL)
- Female: > 50 mg/dL (ideal: > 60 mg/dL)

### LOW HDL is a risk factor for heart disease (even if total cholesterol is normal)

### LOW HDL causes:
- Sedentary lifestyle (biggest factor)
- Smoking
- Obesity and diabetes
- Diet very low in fat
- Genetics

### Lifestyle Tips to RAISE HDL:
- **Exercise is the most powerful tool:** Aerobic exercise (30+ min, 5x/week) can significantly raise HDL.
- **Quit smoking:** Smoking dramatically lowers HDL.
- **Healthy fats:** Modest amounts of olive oil, nuts, and avocados can raise HDL.
- **Lose weight if overweight:** Even 5-10% weight loss can improve HDL significantly.
- **Moderate alcohol may raise HDL** (not a recommendation — risks outweigh benefits for most).

---

## Triglycerides (TG)

### What it measures
The most common type of fat in the body. When you eat more calories than you need, the body converts the excess into triglycerides and stores them.

### Normal Range
| Category | Triglycerides |
|---|---|
| Normal | < 150 mg/dL |
| Borderline High | 150 - 199 mg/dL |
| High | 200 - 499 mg/dL |
| Very High | ≥ 500 mg/dL (pancreatitis risk!) |

### HIGH Triglycerides causes:
- **Refined carbohydrates and sugar** — maida, white rice, sugary drinks, sweets (biggest driver)
- **Alcohol** (even small amounts raise triglycerides significantly)
- **Obesity**
- **Diabetes and insulin resistance**
- **Certain medications:** Beta-blockers, steroids, some diuretics

### Lifestyle Tips if HIGH:
- **Cut refined carbs and sugars aggressively:** This is the single most impactful change for high triglycerides — more so than cutting fat.
- **Eliminate alcohol** during treatment.
- **Omega-3 fatty acids:** Fish (mackerel, sardines, salmon), flaxseeds, walnuts, chia seeds lower triglycerides significantly.
- **Exercise regularly:** 30 minutes of aerobic activity daily.
- **Lose weight:** Even 5% weight loss can lower triglycerides by 20%.

---

## VLDL Cholesterol (Very Low-Density Lipoprotein)

### What it measures
Carries triglycerides from liver to tissues. VLDL is usually calculated (not directly measured):
**VLDL = Triglycerides ÷ 5**

### Normal Range: 5 - 30 mg/dL

### HIGH VLDL mirrors high triglycerides. The lifestyle recommendations for high VLDL are the same as for high triglycerides.

---

## Total Cholesterol / HDL Ratio (Cardiac Risk Ratio)

### What it measures
A composite marker of heart disease risk. Calculated as: Total Cholesterol ÷ HDL Cholesterol.

### Normal Range: < 4.5 (ideal: < 3.5)

### HIGH ratio means
- Even if total cholesterol is only mildly elevated, a low HDL makes the ratio dangerous.
- This ratio can identify cardiovascular risk in people whose total cholesterol is in the "borderline" zone.

---

## LDL/HDL Ratio

### Normal Range: < 3.5 (ideal: < 2.5)
Another composite marker sometimes used. Calculated as: LDL ÷ HDL.

---

## Non-HDL Cholesterol

An emerging risk marker: **Non-HDL = Total Cholesterol - HDL**
This captures all the "bad" cholesterol types collectively (LDL + VLDL).
Target: Less than 130 mg/dL for most people.

---

## Questions to Ask Your Doctor
- "Given all my lipid numbers together, what is my actual 10-year cardiovascular risk?"
- "Should I start a statin, or can we manage my cholesterol with lifestyle changes first?"
- "My triglycerides are very high — should I be concerned about pancreatitis?"
- "Does my thyroid function affect my cholesterol? Should I check my TSH?"
- "Is my cholesterol elevation possibly genetic (familial hypercholesterolemia)? Have any of my family members had early heart attacks?"
