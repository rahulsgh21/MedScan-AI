# Liver Function Test (LFT) Biomarkers

## Overview
The Liver Function Test (LFT) is a group of tests that checks how well your liver is working. The liver performs over 500 functions — filtering toxins, producing bile for digestion, making proteins for blood clotting, and storing energy.

---

## ALT (Alanine Aminotransferase) / SGPT

### What it measures
An enzyme found mainly in the liver. When liver cells are damaged, ALT leaks into the blood.

### Normal Range
- Males: 7 - 56 U/L
- Females: 7 - 45 U/L

### HIGH ALT means
Liver cell damage or inflammation.
- **Common causes:** Fatty liver disease (NAFLD) — very prevalent in India, alcohol consumption, certain medications (paracetamol overdose, statins, anti-TB drugs), hepatitis B or C infection, obesity.
- **Slightly elevated (1-3x normal):** Often fatty liver or medication side effect.
- **Highly elevated (>10x normal):** Acute hepatitis or toxic liver injury — see a doctor immediately.

### LOW ALT
Not typically clinically significant.

### Lifestyle Tips if HIGH:
- **Avoid alcohol completely** during treatment.
- **Reduce refined sugars and fats:** Cut down on maida, white rice, sugary drinks, and fried food — fatty foods worsen NAFLD.
- **Increase physical activity:** Even a 30-minute walk daily can significantly reduce liver fat.
- **Avoid over-the-counter painkillers:** Paracetamol (Crocin/Dolo) in high doses is toxic to the liver.
- **Include:** Leafy greens, turmeric (haldi), amla, and plenty of water.

## AST (Aspartate Aminotransferase) / SGOT

### What it measures
Similar to ALT, but found in the liver AND muscles and heart. Less specific to liver damage.

### Normal Range
- Males: 10 - 40 U/L
- Females: 9 - 32 U/L

### AST/ALT Ratio
Doctors use the ratio to narrow down the cause:
- AST/ALT ratio > 2: Suggests alcoholic liver disease
- AST/ALT ratio < 1: Suggests viral hepatitis or NAFLD

### HIGH AST means
Liver damage, muscle injury (intense exercise can raise it), heart attack, or pancreatitis.

## Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP)

### What it measures
An enzyme present in the liver, bones, and kidneys. ALP is often used to detect liver or bone disease.

### Normal Range
- Adults: 44 - 147 U/L
- Note: Normal values are higher in children and pregnant women.

### HIGH ALP means
- **Liver related:** Bile duct blockage (cholestasis), fatty liver, liver tumors.
- **Bone related:** Bone fractures that are healing, bone disorders like Paget's disease.
- Vitamin D deficiency is a very common cause in India.

### Lifestyle Tips if HIGH (and liver-related):
- Avoid oily, fatty foods.
- Ensure adequate Vitamin D (sunlight exposure 20 min/day, or supplementation as advised by your doctor).

## Bilirubin (Total, Direct, Indirect)

### What it measures
Bilirubin is a yellow pigment produced when red blood cells break down. The liver processes it. High bilirubin causes **jaundice** (yellowing of skin and eyes).

### Normal Range
- Total Bilirubin: 0.1 - 1.2 mg/dL
- Direct (Conjugated): 0.0 - 0.3 mg/dL
- Indirect (Unconjugated): 0.2 - 0.8 mg/dL

### HIGH Total Bilirubin means
- **High Direct Bilirubin:** Liver can't process bilirubin — liver disease, bile duct blockage.
- **High Indirect Bilirubin:** Too many red blood cells breaking down — hemolytic anemia.
- **Both high:** Can indicate hepatitis.

### Lifestyle Tips if HIGH:
- Avoid alcohol completely.
- Stay well-hydrated.
- Avoid heavy, greasy meals.
- Consult a doctor — Jaundice investigation is critical and time-sensitive.

---

## Total Protein

### What it measures
The total amount of two types of blood proteins: albumin and globulin.

### Normal Range: 6.0 - 8.3 g/dL

### LOW Total Protein means
Malnutrition, liver disease (can't make enough protein), kidney disease (losing protein in urine).

---

## Albumin

### What it measures
The main protein made by the liver. It maintains fluid balance in the body and transports hormones, vitamins, and drugs.

### Normal Range: 3.5 - 5.0 g/dL

### LOW Albumin means
Chronic liver disease (like cirrhosis), malnutrition, kidney disease (nephrotic syndrome), or inflammatory states.

### HIGH Albumin means
Dehydration.

### Lifestyle Tips if LOW (nutrition-related):
- Increase protein intake: Eggs, paneer, dal-chawal combinations (complete protein), soy, lean meats.
- Address any underlying cause — chronic liver disease should be discussed with a hepatologist.

---

## Globulin & A/G Ratio

### What it measures
Globulins are proteins involved in the immune system. The Albumin/Globulin (A/G) ratio compares the two.

### Normal A/G Ratio: 1.2 - 2.2

### Interpretation
- **Low A/G ratio (<1.2):** Suggests liver disease, kidney disease, or autoimmune conditions.
- **High A/G ratio (>2.2):** Uncommon; can suggest genetic disorders.

---

## GGT (Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase) / GGTP

### What it measures
A liver enzyme very sensitive to alcohol use and bile duct problems.

### Normal Range
- Males: 8 - 61 U/L
- Females: 5 - 36 U/L

### HIGH GGT means
Alcohol use (even moderate), fatty liver, bile duct disease, or certain medications.

### Questions to Ask Your Doctor
- "Is my SGPT/ALT elevation consistent with fatty liver disease?"
- "Should I get a liver ultrasound to check for fatty liver?"
- "Do I need to be tested for Hepatitis B and Hepatitis C?"
- "Which medications, if any, should I stop or reduce?"
- "Do you recommend a Fibroscan to check for liver fibrosis?"
