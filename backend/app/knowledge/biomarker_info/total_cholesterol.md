# Total Cholesterol

## What It Measures
Total cholesterol is a measure of the total amount of cholesterol in your blood. It includes both "bad" cholesterol (LDL) and "good" cholesterol (HDL), plus other fats. 

## Why is it Important?
Your body needs some cholesterol to build healthy cells, but high levels of cholesterol can increase your risk of heart disease. It builds up in your blood vessels, restricting blood flow.

## Normal Ranges
- **Desirable:** Less than 200 mg/dL
- **Borderline High:** 200 - 239 mg/dL
- **High:** 240 mg/dL and above

## What HIGH Total Cholesterol Means
High total cholesterol indicates an increased risk of cardiovascular issues (heart attacks or strokes). 
- **Common causes:** Diet high in saturated and trans fats (fried foods, processed snacks, high-fat dairy), lack of physical activity, obesity.
- **Other causes:** Genetics (familial hypercholesterolemia), age, smoking, and underlying conditions like diabetes or taking certain medications.

## What LOW Total Cholesterol Means
While rare, extremely low cholesterol is sometimes seen in hyperthyroidism, liver disease, or malabsorption issues. However, lower is generally better for heart health.

## Lifestyle & Diet Recommendations
### If High:
- **Dietary Changes:** 
  - Reduce saturated fats (found in red meat, ghee, butter, and full-fat dairy). 
  - Eliminate trans fats entirely (often found in processed "namkeen" or store-bought baked goods).
  - Increase soluble fiber (oats, beans, lentils, apples, pears).
  - Use heart-healthy oils (mustard oil, olive oil, sunflower oil) in moderation.
- **Exercise:** Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate aerobic activity (brisk walking, cycling, swimming) 5 days a week.
- **Weight Management:** Losing even 5-10% of your body weight can significantly improve cholesterol levels.
- **Quit Smoking:** Smoking lowers "good" HDL cholesterol and damages blood vessels.

## Questions to Ask Your Doctor
- "Based on my LDL, HDL, and Triglyceride breakdown, what is my actual cardiovascular risk?"
- "Can we manage this with lifestyle changes, or do I need statin medications?"
- "Are there any specific dietary guidelines I should follow based on my medical history?"
