# General Health & Indian Dietary Tips

## Hydration
- Aim for 2.5 to 3 liters of water per day, adjusted for climate and physical activity.
- Hydration is key for kidney function and for preventing simple abnormalities in lab reports like slightly elevated Hemoglobin, RBC, or Creatinine due to hemoconcentration.

## Diet
- **Carbohydrates**: Limit white rice and refined flour (maida). Choose complex carbohydrates like whole wheat grains, millets (ragi, bajra), and brown rice.
- **Proteins**: Ensure adequate protein intake. Good vegetarian sources include lentils (dals), chickpeas, paneer, and soya. Non-vegetarian sources include lean chicken, fish, and eggs.
- **Fats**: Avoid trans fats (vanaspati) and limit saturated fats (excess ghee/butter). Use healthy oils like mustard, olive, or groundnut oil.
- **Vegetables**: Fill at least half your plate with non-starchy vegetables at lunch and dinner.
- **Iron Absorption**: Do not consume tea or coffee with or immediately after meals, as tannins inhibit iron absorption. Pair iron-rich foods with vitamin C (like lemon juice) for better absorption.

## Physical Activity
- Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic physical activity per week (e.g., brisk walking, cycling) or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic physical activity.
- Incorporate muscle-strengthening activities on 2 or more days a week.

## Sleep & Stress
- Aim for 7-8 hours of quality sleep per night. Poor sleep can elevate cortisol levels, which in turn can increase blood sugar and blood pressure.
- Practice stress-reducing techniques such as yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises.
